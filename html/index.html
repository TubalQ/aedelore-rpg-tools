Chapter I

The World

The Dragon Gods

Aedelore was shaped by the Dragon Aspects in the wake of the Black Sun's birth. Taninsam brought fire and creation. Tiamat embodied earth and stability. Leviathan commanded air and storms. Tanin'iver offered water and healing. Tatsu governed souls and rebirth. Tohu wielded magic and transformation.

Their influence echoes through every corner of the realm, from the highest peaks to the deepest oceans. The races they created still honor them through various creeds and faiths.

Lands & Cities

Tyralia stands as the grand capital of humanity. Lorenzia shimmers with High Elven magic. The dwarven fortress of Amber's Call endures in frozen mountains. Moon Elves guard their secrets in Feldale. The Sunken City lies in ruin beneath the waves.

The Floating Isles

During the First War, the very fabric of reality tore. Entire landmasses were ripped from the ground and suspended in the sky. These Floating Isles remain—monuments to the devastating power unleashed when the races turned against each other.

Faith & Power

Fourteen distinct religions guide the faithful. The Flame of Taninsam teaches that trials forge the soul. The Silent Hunt follows the Groove Guardian through wild places. The Shattered Path offers solace to outcasts. Each creed shapes how its followers understand their place in Aedelore.

Chapter II

Ages Past

The Fourth Birth

In the time before counting, the Black Sun dreamed within the Void—gathering the essence of three fallen worlds, three great cycles of creation and dissolution. When at last it awakened, six Divine Aspects emerged as vessels of the Eternal Light. Upon a barren world they descended, and through their sacred union was born Aedelore, the Land of Divine Light.

The Fall from Grace

For an age, the veil between mortal and divine was thin, and wonders walked openly upon the land. Yet peace among the unenlightened is but a fleeting shadow. Pride and resentment kindled flames of war, and for fifty years the races bled. When mortal hands refused to stay their violence, the Dragon Gods stirred from slumber. At Nortaq, the priest Lordean offered himself to Tohu's sacred fire—and from that sacrifice, the very earth was torn skyward, giving birth to the Floating Isles.

The Age of Silence

As punishment for the sins of the unworthy, Tohu withdrew magic from the world. The divine light dimmed; what once flowed freely became but a whisper. In the quiet centuries that followed, wounds healed and new truths emerged—some Elves, touched by mortality, became the first Humans. The world rebuilt itself in shadow, awaiting the day when the light might return.

The Rising Shadow

Now ancient forces stir once more. The Orcs and Trolls, hardened by centuries of exile in the outer darkness, have emerged from the Barrowhills. At Rivermount they tested the elven watch—and though the siege was broken, darker whispers spread. Forgotten knowledge resurfaces. The Void grows restless. This is Aedelore as it stands: a world shaped by cycles of light and shadow, awaiting those who would choose which to serve.

Chapter III

The Seven Races

Humans

Versatile and adaptable, humans populate diverse regions. They built grand capitals like Tyralia and East Trade, forming kingdoms through determination and ingenuity.

Dwarves

Stout and resilient craftsmen, dwelling in mountain halls and underground cities. Their mastery of stone and metal is unmatched throughout Aedelore.

High Elves

Gifted with ancient wisdom and magical prowess, High Elves channel the arcane arts from their shimmering city of Lorenzia.

Moon Elves

Attuned to nature and lunar cycles, these enigmatic beings guard their secrets in secluded settlements deep within ancient forests.

Halflings

Small in stature but strong in spirit, Halflings emerged during the Age of Silence. They find joy in simple pleasures and communal living.

Orcs

Fierce and strong, orcs survive in harsh lands through clan bonds and warrior traditions. Their strength and endurance are legendary.

Trolls

Ancient beings with shamanistic traditions, trolls follow the Path of the Ancestors and commune with the Forgotten Loa spirits.

Paths of Power

Warriors

Stalwart defenders clad in armor, masters of blade and shield.

Mages

Wielders of arcane power, manipulating cosmic energies through ancient knowledge.

Druids

Nature's priests, communing with the land and its primal forces.

Hunters

Masters of wilderness survival, tracking prey through any terrain.

Thieves

Shadows incarnate, moving unseen through darkness and deception.

Outcasts

Those who walk no prescribed path, forging their own way through the world.
