Medicinal Plants

Botanical studies by Herbalist Thalia Greenweave documenting the healing flora of Aedelore.

Moonpetal

Classification: Healing Flower | Habitat: Moonlit glades and forest clearings | Rarity: Uncommon

Description

Know that the Moonpetal blooms only beneath the silver gaze of the full moon, its petals unfurling to drink the celestial light. White as fresh snow, touched with luminescent blue at its heart, this sacred flower has been gathered by healers since the First Age.

Properties

When properly prepared as a poultice or tea, Moonpetal accelerates the mending of wounds and soothes fevered brows. The flower must be harvested at the peak of moonrise, for its power wanes with the coming dawn. Thus do wise herbalists venture forth only on nights of the full moon.

Preparation

Steep three petals in spring water heated but not boiled. Add honey of the forest bee to temper the bitter undertones. For wounds, crush fresh petals with rendered fat to create a healing salve.

Dwarvenbane Root

Classification: Medicinal Root | Habitat: Mountain caves and deep valleys | Rarity: Rare

Description

Misnamed by ignorant travelers who believed it harmful to the mountain folk, Dwarvenbane Root is in truth prized by dwarven healers above all other remedies. This gnarled brown root grows in the darkness of mountain caves, drawing sustenance from mineral-rich stone.

Properties

The root possesses remarkable ability to purify the blood of toxins and poisons. Many a miner has been saved from the effects of cave vapors or venomous creatures by swift application of this remedy. It also strengthens the bones when taken regularly over many seasons.

Preparation

The root must be dried for seven days, then ground to fine powder. Mix with strong ale for immediate consumption, or press into tablets with binding agents for later use. Never consume raw, for unprocessed root causes violent illness.

Silverleaf

Classification: Healing Herb | Habitat: Temperate forests and meadows | Rarity: Common

Description

Among the most versatile of healing plants, Silverleaf grows abundantly throughout the realm. Its distinctive silver-green leaves shimmer in sunlight, and even novice herbalists can identify it with ease. The plant grows in clusters, spreading readily across forest floors.

Properties

Silverleaf serves as the foundation for countless remedies. It reduces inflammation, eases pain, and promotes restful sleep. When combined with other herbs, it enhances their potency. Truly, no healer should be without a supply of dried Silverleaf.

Preparation

Fresh leaves may be chewed directly for mild complaints. For greater effect, dry the leaves and brew as tea. The stems yield a sap that, when applied to wounds, prevents corruption of the flesh.

Bloodmoss

Classification: Medicinal Moss | Habitat: Damp caves and northern forests | Rarity: Uncommon

Description

This deep crimson moss clings to rocks in shadowed places where water seeps through stone. Its name derives not from any sinister property, but from its remarkable ability to staunch bleeding. Warriors and hunters prize it as essential equipment for any expedition.

Properties

Applied directly to wounds, Bloodmoss causes immediate clotting. The moss releases compounds that accelerate the body natural healing processes. It also possesses mild antiseptic qualities, reducing the chance of wound corruption.

Preparation

Bloodmoss requires no preparation for emergency use - simply pack it directly onto bleeding wounds. For planned application, dry the moss and grind it into powder that can be stored indefinitely. Rehydrate with clean water before use.

Feverfew

Classification: Medicinal Flower | Habitat: Fields and roadsides | Rarity: Very Common

Description

Small white flowers with yellow centers bloom abundantly in summer, growing wild along roadsides and in cultivated gardens alike. Feverfew has been used by healers since time immemorial, its name speaking plainly to its primary purpose.

Properties

As its name proclaims, Feverfew excels at reducing fevers and breaking the grip of illness. It also eases headaches and migraines when taken at first sign of discomfort. Regular consumption may prevent such ailments entirely.

Preparation

Brew fresh or dried flowers as tea. The taste is bitter, so honey or sweet herbs are often added. For headaches, chew two or three fresh leaves. Some healers create tinctures by steeping flowers in alcohol for extended periods.

Ghostcap Mushroom

Classification: Medicinal Fungus | Habitat: Old growth forests | Rarity: Rare

Description

Translucent and pale as morning mist, the Ghostcap grows only upon the roots of ancient trees. It appears at dawn and dissolves by midday, making collection a matter of precise timing. The fungus glows faintly in darkness, a pale blue luminescence.

Properties

Ghostcap possesses unique ability to soothe ailments of the mind. Those suffering from night terrors, madness, or possession by dark spirits find relief in its application. It also dulls pain without clouding the senses as other remedies might.

Preparation

Must be prepared immediately upon harvest before dissolution. Dry rapidly over low heat, then powder and seal in airtight containers. Administer in small doses mixed with warm milk. Overdose causes vivid hallucinations.

Sunbright

Classification: Healing Flower | Habitat: Sunny hillsides and open meadows | Rarity: Common

Description

Golden petals that follow the sun across the sky mark this cheerful flower. Sunbright grows in great swaths across hillsides, painting the landscape in brilliant yellow. Its stems are thick and its roots deep, making it resilient to drought.

Properties

Sunbright lifts the spirits and combats melancholy. Those who suffer from winter darkness or grief find relief in regular consumption. The flower also aids digestion and stimulates appetite in those weakened by illness.

Preparation

Brew petals as tea for immediate effect. Seeds may be pressed for oil that serves as base for many healing salves. Dried petals retain potency for many months when stored away from moisture.

Willowbark

Classification: Medicinal Bark | Habitat: Riverbanks and wetlands | Rarity: Common

Description

The graceful willow tree grows wherever water flows, its trailing branches dancing in the breeze. The bark of young branches contains powerful medicine, known to healers across all cultures of Aedelore.

Properties

Willowbark serves as one of the most effective remedies for pain and inflammation. It reduces swelling in injured joints, eases the ache of old wounds, and brings relief to those suffering from bone diseases. Its effects last many hours.

Preparation

Strip bark from branches no thicker than a finger. Dry thoroughly and brew as strong tea. The taste is quite bitter. May also be chewed directly for rapid effect, though this method is harsh on the stomach.

Hearthroot

Classification: Medicinal Root | Habitat: Volcanic soil and hot springs | Rarity: Uncommon

Description

In lands where fire meets earth, where hot springs bubble and volcanic soil warms the ground, there grows the Hearthroot. Its orange-red coloring and warmth to the touch make it unmistakable. The plant thrives in conditions that would kill ordinary vegetation.

Properties

Hearthroot warms the body from within, making it invaluable for treating hypothermia and cold-related ailments. It also improves circulation and eases the stiffness of age. Warriors consume it before winter campaigns.

Preparation

Slice the root thin and dry over fire. Brew as tea or chew directly - the warming effect is immediate. May be combined with fatty foods to create long-lasting warmth. Excessive consumption causes sweating and fever.

Dreamvine

Classification: Medicinal Vine | Habitat: Ancient forests and elven lands | Rarity: Rare

Description

Spiraling up the trunks of the oldest trees, Dreamvine bears small purple flowers that release a sweet fragrance at dusk. The elves cultivate it in their sacred groves, for they discovered its properties in ages past.

Properties

Dreamvine induces deep, restful sleep free from nightmares. It is used to treat insomnia, anxiety, and the trauma of battle. Some seers use it to enhance prophetic dreams, though this practice carries risks.

Preparation

Harvest flowers at twilight for maximum potency. Dry gently and brew as tea to be consumed before sleep. The leaves may be burned as incense for milder effect. Never combine with alcohol, for the combination may prove fatal.

Magical Flora

Arcane botanical studies by Sage Meridian of the Azure Tower documenting plants with supernatural properties.

Starbloom

Classification: Celestial Flower | Habitat: High mountain peaks | Rarity: Very Rare | Magical Affinity: Divination, Light

Description

Know that upon the highest peaks, where mortal feet seldom tread and the air grows thin as whispered secrets, there blooms the Starbloom. Its petals shift through colors unknown to ordinary flowers - not merely blue or violet, but hues that exist between colors, shades that the eye struggles to name.

Magical Properties

The Starbloom serves as a conduit between the mortal realm and the celestial spheres. Mages who consume its nectar report visions of distant places and future events. The flower is essential in brewing potions of foresight and creating scrying implements.

Harvesting Notes

Must be gathered during meteor showers or eclipses for full potency. The flower screams silently when cut - a psychic shriek that disturbs sensitive minds. Handle with silver tools only.

Voidthorn

Classification: Abyssal Plant | Habitat: Places touched by the Void | Rarity: Extremely Rare | Magical Affinity: Void Magic, Necromancy

Description

Where the Void has touched the world and left its mark, there sometimes grows the Voidthorn. Black as the space between stars, its thorns drip with liquid shadow. No natural light falls upon it - rather, it seems to drink illumination from its surroundings.

Magical Properties

Essential component in void magic rituals and certain necromantic preparations. The thorns, when powdered, allow temporary sight into the spaces between worlds. Extremely dangerous to handle without proper protections.

Harvesting Notes

Approach only with wards against void corruption active. Use obsidian blades to cut. Never allow the shadow-sap to touch bare skin. Store in lead-lined containers away from living plants, which it will corrupt.

Flamelily

Classification: Elemental Flower | Habitat: Volcanic regions and fire-touched lands | Rarity: Rare | Magical Affinity: Fire, Transmutation

Description

The Flamelily burns without consuming itself, its petals actual flames given botanical form. It requires no soil, growing directly from rock heated by volcanic activity. The flower produces no seeds - new specimens simply ignite into existence where conditions permit.

Magical Properties

Primary ingredient in potions of fire resistance and fire-breathing elixirs. Enchanted weapons benefit from Flamelily essence, gaining properties of flame. The flower can also be used to create ever-burning torches.

Harvesting Notes

Obviously requires immunity or resistance to fire for safe collection. The flame does not transfer to containers - the flower simply extinguishes if removed carelessly. Must be transported in specially enchanted crystal vessels.

Frostrose

Classification: Elemental Flower | Habitat: Eternal glaciers and frozen wastes | Rarity: Rare | Magical Affinity: Ice, Preservation

Description

Crystalline and beautiful, the Frostrose grows in the deepest cold where lesser plants would shatter. Each petal is formed of living ice, yet the flower possesses warmth at its heart - a paradox that defies natural law. It chimes softly in the wind.

Magical Properties

Used in creating potions of cold resistance and preservation magic. Items treated with Frostrose essence resist decay and aging. The flower is also essential for certain cryomantic rituals and the creation of ice elementals.

Harvesting Notes

The flower melts if brought to temperate climates without magical preservation. Harvest during the deepest part of winter for maximum potency. The chiming can attract ice elementals - harvest quickly.

Spiritveil

Classification: Ethereal Plant | Habitat: Haunted places and ancient burial grounds | Rarity: Uncommon | Magical Affinity: Necromancy, Spirit Magic

Description

Semi-transparent and wavering like heat haze, Spiritveil grows where the boundary between life and death has worn thin. The plant exists partially in the spirit realm, visible only to those with magical sight or during certain phases of the moon.

Magical Properties

Allows communication with the dead when properly prepared. Essential component in ghost-binding rituals and exorcisms. Can be used to create temporary incorporeality or to anchor wandering spirits.

Harvesting Notes

Must be gathered on nights when spirits walk freely. Use consecrated silver sickles. The plant may attract spirits during harvest - prepare appropriate protections. Store in vessels inscribed with spirit-warding runes.

Manawort

Classification: Arcane Herb | Habitat: Ley line intersections | Rarity: Uncommon | Magical Affinity: Raw Magic, Enhancement

Description

Where ley lines cross and magical energy pools, Manawort flourishes. The herb glows with soft blue light, pulsing in rhythm with the magical currents beneath the earth. Its leaves are warm to the touch, tingling with contained power.

Magical Properties

Consumption restores depleted magical reserves. Essential ingredient in mana potions used by spellcasters throughout Aedelore. Can also be used to enhance the potency of other magical preparations.

Harvesting Notes

Grows only at ley line intersections, which must be located through magical means. Harvest during magical high tides for maximum potency. The plant resists being transplanted - it simply withers away from its power source.

Shadowmoss

Classification: Dark Flora | Habitat: Places of permanent darkness | Rarity: Uncommon | Magical Affinity: Shadow, Illusion

Description

Shadowmoss grows where light has never touched - the deepest caves, sealed crypts, and magically darkened chambers. It is darkness given vegetable form, soft as velvet and black as a moonless night. It retreats from light like a living creature.

Magical Properties

Essential for invisibility potions and shadow magic rituals. Can be used to create areas of magical darkness or to enhance stealth enchantments. Some assassin guilds cultivate it for their own purposes.

Harvesting Notes

Must be harvested in complete darkness - any light destroys its magical properties instantly. Use touch alone to locate and gather. Store in lightless containers. The moss whispers in darkness, sharing secrets of shadow.

Lifeblossom

Classification: Sacred Flower | Habitat: Holy places and temples of Elarion | Rarity: Very Rare | Magical Affinity: Healing, Life

Description

Golden-white and radiant with divine energy, the Lifeblossom grows only in places blessed by the divine. Temples to Elarion often cultivate them in sacred gardens, for the flower embodies the life-giving aspect of the Radiant One.

Magical Properties

The most potent healing agent known to herbalism. Can cure diseases that resist all other treatments and heal wounds that would otherwise prove fatal. In the hands of skilled healers, it can even restore those on the threshold of death.

Harvesting Notes

May only be gathered by those pure of heart - the flower withers at the touch of evil. Must be used fresh, as it loses potency rapidly after harvest. Never sells on the open market - temples guard their supplies jealously.

Thundercap

Classification: Elemental Fungus | Habitat: Storm-struck highlands | Rarity: Rare | Magical Affinity: Lightning, Storm

Description

This mushroom grows where lightning has struck, feeding on the residual electrical energy. Its cap crackles with contained static, and touching it produces a sharp shock. During storms, Thundercaps glow with inner light and release spores on the wind.

Magical Properties

Essential for lightning-based magic and storm-calling rituals. Can be used to create shock-enchanted weapons or to brew potions that grant temporary lightning powers. Storm mages prize it above most other reagents.

Best gathered immediately after lightning strikes. Wear insulating gloves - the shock can stun or kill. Transport in glass containers to prevent discharge. Potency fades within a fortnight unless preserved magically.

Mindweed

Mindweed grows where strong emotions or thoughts have left their mark upon the world - battlefields of great sorrow, places of profound meditation, or sites of collective madness. The plant seems aware, turning toward those who approach.

Magical Properties

Enhances mental abilities and psychic powers. Used in telepathy rituals, memory manipulation, and mental defense preparations. Can also be used to create potions that temporarily boost intelligence or grant limited mind-reading.

Common Plants

Agricultural records by Fieldmaster Bram Thornbury documenting the everyday flora that sustains civilization.

Golden Wheat

Classification: Grain Crop | Habitat: Temperate farmland | Uses: Food staple

Description

The golden wheat of Aedelore feeds nations. Tall stalks bend heavy with grain come harvest time, painting the fields in shimmering gold. This crop forms the foundation of civilization, for without bread there can be no lasting settlement.

Cultivation

Plant in early spring after the last frost. Requires well-drained soil and adequate rainfall. Harvest in late summer when stalks turn fully golden. Rotate with legumes to maintain soil health.

Uses

Ground into flour for bread, pastries, and countless other foods. The stalks serve as bedding for livestock and thatching for roofs. Fermented grain produces ale and spirits.

Riverside Reed

Classification: Wetland Plant | Habitat: Rivers, marshes, and lakeshores | Uses: Crafting material

Description

Tall and flexible, reeds grow in dense stands along every waterway in the realm. They sway in the wind like dancers, their rustling a familiar sound to all who dwell near water. The plants spread rapidly, reclaiming land from lakes and rivers.

Cultivation

Rarely cultivated, as wild stands provide ample supply. If needed, transplant root sections to wet soil. Once established, reeds require no care and spread of their own accord.

Uses

Woven into baskets, mats, and simple furniture. Bundled for roofing material in rural areas. The hollow stems serve as drinking straws and simple musical instruments. Young shoots are edible when boiled.

Oakheart Acorn

Classification: Tree Seed | Habitat: Temperate forests | Uses: Food, cultivation

Description

The mighty oaks of Aedelore produce acorns in abundance each autumn. These humble nuts, capped with their distinctive cups, contain the potential for trees that will stand for centuries. Squirrels and other creatures compete with humans for the harvest.

Cultivation

Acorns germinate readily when planted in autumn. Oaks grow slowly but steadily, requiring patience measured in decades. A single tree may produce thousands of acorns annually once mature.

Uses

Processed to remove bitter tannins, acorns yield nutritious flour. Raw acorns feed livestock, especially pigs. Oak timber serves countless construction purposes, and the bark provides tanning agents for leather.

Wild Carrot

Classification: Root Vegetable | Habitat: Meadows and field edges | Uses: Food

Description

The ancestor of cultivated carrots grows wild throughout Aedelore. Its white root is smaller and tougher than garden varieties, but perfectly edible. White flower clusters bloom in summer, resembling delicate lace.

Cultivation

Wild carrots require no cultivation, growing wherever soil is disturbed. Garden varieties have been bred for larger, sweeter, orange roots. Biennial - produces flowers and seeds in second year.

Uses

Roots eaten raw or cooked. Seeds used as spice with flavor similar to caraway. Flowers attract beneficial insects to gardens. Note: resembles deadly poison hemlock - ensure proper identification.

Clover

Classification: Fodder Plant | Habitat: Meadows and pastures | Uses: Livestock feed, soil improvement

Description

Three-leafed clover carpets meadows across the land, its round flower heads humming with bees throughout summer. Children search for rare four-leafed specimens, believed to bring good fortune. The plant grows low and dense.

Cultivation

Sow seeds in spring or early autumn. Clover fixes nitrogen in soil, making it invaluable for crop rotation. Mow periodically to encourage dense growth. Self-seeds readily once established.

Uses

Primary fodder for cattle, sheep, and horses. Excellent honey source - clover honey is prized for its mild flavor. Improves soil fertility when plowed under. Young leaves edible in salads.

Flax

Classification: Fiber Crop | Habitat: Temperate farmland | Uses: Cloth, oil

Description

Slender stems topped with delicate blue flowers wave in summer breezes. Flax has been cultivated since the earliest days of civilization, providing both fiber for cloth and seeds for oil. It grows in dense stands, the stems rising to waist height.

Cultivation

Plant seeds densely in spring. Requires adequate moisture and well-drained soil. Harvest entire plant before seeds mature for fiber, or allow to mature for seed harvest.

Uses

Processed stems yield linen fiber for cloth. Seeds pressed for linseed oil, used in cooking, wood finishing, and paint making. Seed cake feeds livestock. Blue flowers make pleasant garden additions.

Barley

Classification: Grain Crop | Habitat: Temperate to cool farmland | Uses: Food, brewing

Description

Hardier than wheat, barley grows where other grains struggle. Its distinctive long awns give ripening fields a bearded appearance. This grain sustained early civilizations and remains essential for both food and drink production.

Cultivation

Plant in spring or autumn depending on variety. Tolerates poorer soil and shorter growing seasons than wheat. Harvest when grains are hard and stems dry.

Uses

Malted barley is essential for brewing ale and spirits. Whole grains cooked as porridge or added to soups. Pearl barley, with hull removed, is a common food grain. Straw used for bedding and animal feed.

Hemp

Classification: Fiber Crop | Habitat: Varied climates | Uses: Rope, cloth, paper

Description

Tall and fast-growing, hemp reaches impressive heights in a single season. Its distinctive palmate leaves and fibrous stalks have made it indispensable for rope-making since ships first sailed. The plant adapts to many conditions.

Cultivation

Direct sow in late spring. Grows rapidly in most soils. Requires little attention beyond adequate water. Harvest when lower leaves yellow. Can exhaust soil if grown repeatedly in same location.

Uses

Strong fibers make superior rope and canvas. Processed into paper that lasts centuries. Seeds yield nutritious oil. In some cultures, certain varieties used for medicinal or ritual purposes.

Apple Tree

Classification: Fruit Tree | Habitat: Temperate orchards | Uses: Food

Description

Apple trees grace orchards throughout the temperate lands, their spring blossoms a celebration of renewal and their autumn fruit a bounty of sweetness. Countless varieties exist, from tiny sour crabs to large sweet dessert apples.

Cultivation

Propagate by grafting to maintain variety characteristics. Plant in well-drained soil with good sun exposure. Prune annually to maintain shape and productivity. Most varieties require cross-pollination.

Uses

Fresh eating, cooking, drying, and cider production. Preserved as sauce, butter, or dried rings. Cider vinegar serves culinary and cleaning purposes. Wood prized for smoking meats and carving.

Grapevine

Classification: Fruit Vine | Habitat: Warm, sunny hillsides | Uses: Wine, food

Description

Climbing and spreading, grapevines transform sunny slopes into productive vineyards. The fruit hangs in heavy clusters, ranging from pale green to deep purple depending on variety. Winemaking defines many regional cultures.

Cultivation

Plant rooted cuttings in well-drained soil on south-facing slopes. Train on trellises or arbors. Prune heavily each winter. Requires several years to reach full production.

Uses

Fresh table grapes, dried as raisins, and fermented into wine of endless variety. Grape juice provides non-alcoholic beverage. Leaves stuffed with savory fillings in some cuisines. Seeds yield culinary oil.

Dangerous Plants

Warning catalog by Poisoner-Scholar Vesper Nighthollow documenting hazardous flora to be avoided or handled with extreme care.

Deathbell

Classification: Deadly Poison | Habitat: Shaded woodlands | Danger Level: Lethal

Description

Heed well this warning: the beautiful purple bells that hang from this innocent-seeming plant conceal death itself. Every part of the Deathbell carries lethal poison - root, stem, leaf, and flower alike. Even touching it with bare skin risks absorption of its toxins.

Effects

Ingestion causes rapid heartbeat followed by heart failure. Skin contact produces numbness and, in sufficient quantity, paralysis. There is no known antidote - only immediate purging offers hope of survival, and even then death may follow.

Identification

Purple bell-shaped flowers drooping from arching stems. Leaves are oval and slightly fuzzy. Often found in dappled shade beneath trees. Entire plant is somewhat wilted-looking even when healthy.

Strangler Vine

Classification: Predatory Plant | Habitat: Dense jungles and ancient forests | Danger Level: Dangerous

Description

This vine does not wait passively for prey - it hunts. Sensing warmth and movement, the Strangler Vine lashes out with alarming speed, wrapping around creatures and constricting until life departs. Solitary vines pose little threat, but dense growths have claimed many lives.

Effects

The vine constricts with surprising strength, capable of suffocating humans. Thorns along its length inject a paralytic compound that prevents struggle. Victims are slowly absorbed as the vine feeds.

Identification

Thick, rope-like vines that twitch slightly even without wind. Covered in fine thorns. Often draped between trees at head height. Red sap bleeds when cut. Clusters of undigested bones beneath mature specimens.

Corpseflower

Classification: Toxic Bloom | Habitat: Swamps and graveyards | Danger Level: Moderate

Description

Named for its nauseating stench of rotting flesh, the Corpseflower grows where death has enriched the soil. Its massive mottled purple bloom can reach the size of a shield. The smell attracts carrion insects, which pollinate it.

Effects

The stench alone causes nausea and disorientation. Pollen produces hallucinations and temporary blindness. Touching the flower results in blistering rashes that heal slowly and scar.

Identification

Impossible to miss - the smell announces it from great distance. Single enormous flower rising from the ground on a thick stalk. Mottled purple and red coloring resembling rotting meat. Usually surrounded by flies and beetles.

Witchwood

Classification: Cursed Tree | Habitat: Blighted areas | Danger Level: Dangerous

Description

These twisted, blackened trees grow where dark magic has corrupted the land. Their branches reach like grasping claws, and their bark weeps a viscous black sap. No bird nests in Witchwood, no animal shelters beneath it, for all living things sense its malevolence.

Effects

Proximity causes feelings of dread and despair. Extended exposure results in nightmares and paranoia. The sap curses those it touches, bringing persistent misfortune. Burning Witchwood releases toxic, hallucinogenic smoke.

Identification

Blackened, twisted trunk and branches. No leaves or only withered, gray ones. Black sap oozing from bark. Area around tree is barren - no other plants grow within several paces. Often groans or whispers when wind blows.

Bloodthorn

Classification: Parasitic Plant | Habitat: Forest edges and clearings | Danger Level: Moderate

Description

Beautiful red berries lure the unwary to this thorny bush, but the Bloodthorn takes more than it gives. Its thorns are hollow, designed to pierce flesh and drink blood. A large enough patch can drain a trapped creature completely.

Effects

Thorns pierce deeply and resist removal, breaking off in wounds. Anticoagulant compounds prevent clotting, causing persistent bleeding. Larger wounds may become infected. Berries are mildly toxic, causing stomach cramps.

Identification

Dense, thorny bushes with distinctive red berries. Thorns are unusually long and slightly curved. Often found with animal remains tangled in branches. Grows aggressively, spreading to block paths.

Nightshade

Classification: Deadly Poison | Habitat: Woodlands and waste ground | Danger Level: Lethal

Description

Also called Belladonna or Devils Berries, Nightshade has claimed countless lives throughout history. Its shiny black berries taste sweet, making them especially dangerous to children. Every part of the plant is toxic.

Effects

Dilated pupils, rapid heartbeat, hallucinations, and convulsions precede death. Even small amounts can be fatal. Some use minute doses medicinally, but the margin between therapeutic and lethal is razor-thin.

Identification

Bushy plant with oval leaves and dull purple flowers. Berries are shiny black, about the size of cherries. Often grows in disturbed soil near settlements. Resembles edible plants to the untrained eye.

Blightcap

Classification: Toxic Fungus | Habitat: Decaying matter in dark places | Danger Level: Moderate to Severe

Description

This sickly yellow mushroom releases clouds of spores when disturbed. The spores carry a wasting disease that afflicts those who breathe them. Blightcap colonies can render entire cave systems uninhabitable.

Effects

Spore inhalation causes a progressive lung disease called Miners Blight. Victims cough blood and gradually suffocate over weeks or months. No cure exists, though symptoms can be managed.

Identification

Sickly yellow-green cap with white gills. Grows in clusters on rotting wood or corpses. Gives off faint sulfurous smell. Cap releases visible yellow spore cloud when touched or when mature.

Madwort

Classification: Psychoactive Poison | Habitat: Disturbed soil, roadsides | Danger Level: Moderate

Description

Unremarkable in appearance, Madwort has driven countless people to violent insanity. The plant was once used by berserker cults to induce battle-fury, but such practices are now outlawed throughout civilized lands.

Effects

Ingestion causes paranoid delusions, uncontrollable rage, and complete loss of pain sensation. Users attack friends and foes alike. Effects last hours and leave no memory. Repeated use causes permanent mental damage.

Identification

Nondescript weed with small white flowers. Gray-green leaves with serrated edges. Bitter taste that is usually disguised when administered to victims. Grows commonly in waste ground.

Sundew Giant

Classification: Carnivorous Plant | Habitat: Bogs and wetlands | Danger Level: Dangerous

Description

While ordinary sundews catch insects, the Giant Sundew of Aedelore catches far larger prey. Its sticky tentacles can immobilize creatures up to the size of a dog, slowly digesting them over days. Larger specimens have been known to trap humans.

Effects

Adhesive secretion is nearly impossible to remove without proper solvents. Digestive enzymes cause severe chemical burns. Struggle only spreads the adhesive further. Drowning in bog water often kills victims before digestion.

Identification

Large rosette of reddish leaves covered in glistening tentacles. Each tentacle tipped with sticky droplet resembling dew. Grows in open boggy areas. Often surrounded by bones of previous victims.

Serpentroot

Classification: Animated Hazard | Habitat: Dark forests | Danger Level: Moderate

Description

Serpentroot earned its name from its disturbing ability to move. The roots of this plant slither through soil like snakes, tripping travelers and dragging small creatures underground. Whether this is true sentience or mere reflex remains debated.

Effects

Roots actively attempt to entangle legs and drag victims down. Constriction causes bruising and broken bones. Victims pulled underground suffocate. The plant seems drawn to vibration and body heat.

Identification

Above-ground portion is unremarkable - small purple flowers on leafy stems. Ground around plant shows disturbed soil and surface roots that occasionally twitch. Area often littered with small animal bones.

Domesticated Animals

Husbandry guide by Stablemaster Aldwyn Bridger documenting the animals that serve civilization.

Draft Horse

Classification: Work Animal | Size: Large | Lifespan: 20-25 years

Description

The draft horses of Aedelore are massive, powerful beasts bred for strength rather than speed. Standing taller than a man at the shoulder, these gentle giants pull plows, haul wagons, and perform countless heavy tasks that would break lesser animals.

Temperament

Despite their imposing size, draft horses are notably calm and patient. They bond strongly with their handlers and work willingly. Rarely startled, they provide steady reliability in all conditions.

Care Requirements

Require substantial food - grain supplemented with hay. Need spacious stabling and regular exercise. Hooves require frequent care due to their weight. Prone to leg injuries if overworked.

Breeds

Dawnshire Grey - pale coats, exceptional endurance. Ironwood Black - strongest breed, used in mining. Mountain Draft - shorter but surefooted for hilly terrain.

Riding Horse

Classification: Transportation | Size: Medium-Large | Lifespan: 25-30 years

Description

Bred for speed, stamina, and grace, riding horses carry travelers across the realm. Lighter than draft breeds, these horses combine beauty with athleticism. A good riding horse represents significant investment and status.

Temperament

More spirited than draft horses, requiring confident handling. Form strong bonds with regular riders. Can be trained for various tasks from casual travel to warfare.

Care Requirements

Moderate food requirements. Need daily exercise and grooming. Regular hoof care essential. Benefit from companionship of other horses.

Breeds

Velantian Courser - fastest breed, favored by messengers. Thornwood Hunter - sturdy and brave, popular with nobility. Plains Runner - exceptional endurance for long journeys.

Dairy Cow

Classification: Livestock | Size: Large | Lifespan: 15-20 years

Description

The foundation of pastoral farming, dairy cows provide milk that sustains countless families. These placid animals graze contentedly in pastures, requiring minimal supervision. A single cow can support a small family with her daily yield.

Temperament

Calm and docile, following established routines. Recognize their handlers and may show affection. Herd animals that become distressed when isolated.

Care Requirements

Require extensive pasture or hay supply. Need shelter from extreme weather. Must be milked daily when lactating. Regular health checks for common ailments.

Products

Fresh milk, cream, butter, and cheese. Aged animals provide beef and leather. Manure fertilizes fields. Calves sold or raised for herd replacement.

Wool Sheep

Classification: Livestock | Size: Medium | Lifespan: 10-12 years

Description

Sheep provide wool for clothing, meat for sustenance, and milk for cheese. They graze marginal land unsuitable for crops, converting grass into valuable resources. Large flocks paint hillsides white across the realm.

Temperament

Gregarious and timid, following the flock instinctively. Easily panicked by predators. Require protection from wolves and other threats.

Care Requirements

Need grazing land with shelter options. Shearing required at least annually. Susceptible to various parasites and foot problems. Dogs essential for managing larger flocks.

Products

Wool sheared annually for textiles. Meat prized for its flavor. Milk makes distinctive cheeses. Lanolin from wool used in ointments and leather treatment.

Farm Pig

Classification: Livestock | Size: Medium-Large | Lifespan: 15-20 years

Description

Intelligent and adaptable, pigs convert scraps and forage into meat with remarkable efficiency. They root through forests for acorns and truffles, and consume kitchen waste that would otherwise be lost. No part of a pig goes unused.

Temperament

Surprisingly intelligent, capable of learning commands. Social animals that enjoy company. Can become aggressive if mistreated or during mating season.

Care Requirements

Thrive on varied diet including scraps and forage. Need mud or water for temperature regulation. Require sturdy fencing to prevent escape. Social needs met by group housing.

Products

Pork fresh or preserved as ham, bacon, and sausage. Fat rendered into lard for cooking. Skin becomes leather. Bristles used for brushes. Truly, nothing is wasted.

Chicken

Classification: Poultry | Size: Small | Lifespan: 5-8 years

Description

Found in virtually every farm and many urban dwellings, chickens provide eggs daily and meat when needed. These hardy birds require little space and thrive on scraps and insects. A small flock provides protein security for any household.

Temperament

Establish strict pecking order within flocks. Roosters protective but sometimes aggressive. Hens reliably lay if properly cared for.

Care Requirements

Need secure housing to protect from predators. Require grit for digestion. Supplemental feed during winter when foraging limited. Clean water essential.

Products

Eggs daily from laying hens. Meat from excess roosters and older hens. Feathers for bedding and quills. Manure excellent for gardens.

Goat

Classification: Livestock | Size: Small-Medium | Lifespan: 12-15 years

Description

Goats thrive where other livestock struggle - rocky hillsides, scrubland, and areas of sparse vegetation. Their ability to browse on brush and weeds makes them valuable for land clearing as well as their products.

Temperament

Curious and clever, known for escaping inadequate fencing. Playful and social. Can be stubborn but respond to patient handling.

Care Requirements

Need varied browse including brush and weeds. Require sturdy, high fencing. Shelter from rain essential - goats dislike getting wet. Regular hoof trimming necessary.

Products

Milk for drinking and distinctive cheeses. Meat lean and flavorful. Hair from some breeds woven into fabric. Hides make supple leather.

Herding Dog

Classification: Working Animal | Size: Medium | Lifespan: 12-15 years

Description

Essential partners to shepherds, herding dogs manage flocks with intelligence that seems almost supernatural. A single well-trained dog can control hundreds of sheep, anticipating their movements and responding to subtle commands.

Temperament

Intensely focused and eager to work. Form unbreakable bonds with handlers. Need mental stimulation - bored herding dogs become destructive.

Care Requirements

Require extensive daily exercise and work. Thrive on training and tasks. Need quality food to maintain energy. Regular grooming depending on coat type.

Breeds

Moorland Collie - classic black and white, exceptional intelligence. Dawnshire Shepherd - larger, also guards against predators. Mountain Cur - independent, works with minimal direction.

Hunting Hawk

Classification: Hunting Animal | Size: Small-Medium | Lifespan: 15-20 years

Description

The ancient art of falconry remains popular among nobility, and trained hawks assist hunters of all classes. These fierce predators form partnerships with their handlers, returning from the sky with game that would otherwise escape.

Temperament

Wild at heart despite training. Respond to patience and consistency. Never truly tame but can form working relationships with skilled handlers.

Care Requirements

Require specialized housing and equipment. Need regular hunting exercise. Diet of fresh meat. Molting periods require special care. Training is lengthy process requiring dedication.

Types

Goshawk - powerful, takes rabbits and game birds. Peregrine - fastest, specializes in aerial pursuit. Merlin - small, hunts songbirds and requires less space.

Mule

Classification: Work Animal | Size: Medium-Large | Lifespan: 30-40 years

Description

The offspring of horse and donkey combines the best qualities of both parents. Mules possess remarkable strength, endurance, and surefootedness. Though they cannot reproduce, their practical virtues make them invaluable working animals.

Temperament

Intelligent and cautious - they refuse to endanger themselves, unlike horses. Patient workers but stubborn when pushed beyond their limits. Form strong bonds with handlers.

Care Requirements

Require less food than horses of similar size. Hardy and disease-resistant. Need regular hoof care. Thrive on consistent routine and fair treatment.

Uses

Pack transport on difficult terrain. Pulling plows in rocky soil. Mining operations where their caution prevents accidents. Long-distance trade caravans.

Forest Wolf

Classification: Predator | Size: Large | Habitat: Temperate forests | Threat Level: Moderate

Description

The wolves of Aedelore forests are cunning pack hunters, their howls echoing through the trees on cold nights. Gray-furred and yellow-eyed, they command both fear and respect from those who share their territory.

Behavior

Hunt in coordinated packs led by alpha pairs. Primarily prey on deer and other large herbivores. Rarely attack humans unless starving or defending pups. Territorial, marking boundaries with scent.

Ecological Role

Essential for controlling deer and boar populations. Their presence shapes the behavior of prey species, keeping forests healthy. Where wolves are hunted out, herbivores strip vegetation.

Great Bear

Classification: Omnivore | Size: Very Large | Habitat: Forests and mountains | Threat Level: High

Description

Standing taller than a man when reared on hind legs, the Great Bear commands respect throughout Aedelore. Brown to black in color, these massive creatures possess strength sufficient to kill with a single blow.

Behavior

Solitary except during mating or when mothers raise cubs. Omnivorous - eating berries, fish, insects, and occasionally larger prey. Hibernate through winter in prepared dens. Extremely dangerous when surprised or protecting cubs.

Ecological Role

Apex predator that shapes forest ecology. Spread seeds through droppings. Their fishing activities bring ocean nutrients inland. Abandoned dens provide shelter for smaller animals.

Wild Boar

Classification: Omnivore | Size: Medium-Large | Habitat: Forests and scrubland | Threat Level: Moderate

Description

Bristle-coated and tusked, wild boars root through forest floors in search of food. These aggressive creatures fear little, charging threats with surprising speed. Many hunters have learned too late that boars are not easy prey.

Behavior

Live in groups called sounders, led by dominant sows. Omnivorous, eating roots, nuts, fungi, and small animals. Males solitary except during mating. Will attack when cornered or protecting young.

Ecological Role

Their rooting aerates soil and spreads seeds. Control insect populations. Provide food for larger predators. Can become pests when populations grow unchecked.

Red Deer

Classification: Herbivore | Size: Large | Habitat: Forests and meadows | Threat Level: Low

Description

The noble red deer graces forests and meadows throughout Aedelore. Stags carry magnificent antlers that grow larger each year, shed and regrown annually. Their autumn rutting calls echo through misty mornings.

Behavior

Herds led by dominant stags during rut, otherwise by experienced does. Graze at dawn and dusk, resting during day. Flee from predators rather than fight, though stags will defend harems and does protect fawns.

Ecological Role

Primary prey for large predators. Grazing maintains meadow ecosystems. Shed antlers provide minerals for smaller animals. Valued by hunters for meat, hide, and antlers.

Mountain Lion

Classification: Predator | Size: Large | Habitat: Mountains and forests | Threat Level: High

Description

Tawny-coated and silent, mountain lions are ambush predators of supreme skill. They stalk prey through rocky terrain with patience measured in hours, then strike with explosive speed. Solitary and secretive, they are rarely seen.

Behavior

Strictly solitary except during mating. Hunt by stalking and pouncing. Cache large kills and return to feed over several days. Territorial, with ranges spanning many miles.

Ecological Role

Control deer and goat populations in mountainous regions. Their presence keeps prey animals alert and mobile, preventing overgrazing. Carcasses feed scavengers throughout their territory.

Golden Eagle

Classification: Predator | Size: Medium (Large wingspan) | Habitat: Mountains and open country | Threat Level: Low to humans

Description

Ruling the skies above Aedelore, golden eagles soar on thermals with wingspans wider than a man is tall. Their keen eyes spot prey from impossible heights, and their dive speed defies belief.

Behavior

Mate for life, returning to the same nests yearly. Hunt rabbits, marmots, and young deer. Fiercely territorial during breeding season. Will strike at threats near their nests.

Ecological Role

Control populations of small mammals. Nests provide habitat for smaller birds after abandonment. Considered sacred or symbolic by many cultures. Occasionally trained for hunting.

River Otter

Classification: Aquatic Predator | Size: Medium | Habitat: Rivers and lakes | Threat Level: None

Description

Playful and sleek, river otters bring joy to those fortunate enough to observe them. Their waterproof fur and webbed feet make them supreme swimmers, hunting fish with acrobatic grace.

Behavior

Highly social, living in family groups. Hunt fish, crayfish, and frogs. Known for playful behavior - sliding, wrestling, and playing with objects. Active year-round, even in winter.

Ecological Role

Indicate healthy waterways - otters disappear from polluted waters. Control fish and crayfish populations. Their slides and dens modify riverbanks. Beloved by all who encounter them.

Shadowcat

Classification: Predator | Size: Medium-Large | Habitat: Dense forests | Threat Level: Moderate

Description

Nearly invisible in forest shadows, the Shadowcat is a spotted predator of exceptional stealth. Its coat pattern breaks up its outline perfectly, and it moves without sound. Many travelers pass within feet of watching Shadowcats without ever knowing.

Behavior

Strictly nocturnal. Ambush hunters that drop from trees onto prey. Solitary and territorial. Avoid humans when possible but will attack if threatened or starving.

Ecological Role

Control populations of deer, boar, and smaller predators. Their presence indicates healthy, mature forest. Pelts highly valued but hunting is difficult and dangerous.

Elkhorn Stag

Classification: Herbivore | Size: Very Large | Habitat: Northern forests and tundra | Threat Level: Low to Moderate

Description

Larger than red deer, Elkhorn Stags carry antlers of tremendous size - some spanning wider than a man can stretch his arms. These majestic creatures roam the northern reaches, their breath steaming in cold air.

Behavior

Migratory, following seasonal food sources. Live in large herds for protection. Stags compete fiercely during rut, their antlers clashing in tests of strength. Can be aggressive if approached during this period.

Ecological Role

Keystone species of northern ecosystems. Their grazing shapes vegetation patterns. Provide food for predators and carrion eaters. Antlers collected for tools and trade goods.

Rockbadger

Classification: Omnivore | Size: Small-Medium | Habitat: Rocky hills and mountains | Threat Level: Low

Description

Stocky and fearless, rockbadgers inhabit stone-strewn terrain throughout Aedelore. Their powerful claws excavate dens in the hardest ground, and their disposition makes even larger predators think twice before confrontation.

Behavior

Nocturnal, emerging at dusk to forage. Omnivorous - eating roots, insects, small animals, and carrion. Famously aggressive when threatened, standing ground against much larger opponents.

Ecological Role

Their extensive burrow systems provide homes for many other species. Control insect populations. Turn soil through digging. Admired for their courage despite small size.
