Guilds & Organizations

The Merchants Guild

Compiled by Guildmaster Archivist Tobias Ledger

FoundedThird Age, Year 234
HeadquartersThe Golden Exchange, Velantor
InfluenceRealm-wide

History

Know that commerce binds the realm as surely as roads and rivers. The Merchants Guild arose when traders sought protection from bandits and fair dealing among themselves. What began as mutual aid has grown into the most powerful economic force in Aedelore.

Structure

The Grand Merchant Council governs from Velantor, with regional chapters in every major city. Membership requires sponsorship and substantial fees. In return, members gain trade route protection, dispute arbitration, and access to guild warehouses.

Activities

Controls most legitimate trade. Sets prices and standards. Maintains trade roads. Operates banks and money-lending services. Has been known to destroy competition through legal and less savory means.

The Mages Collegium

Compiled by Guildmaster Archivist Tobias Ledger

FoundedSecond Age
HeadquartersThe Azure Tower
InfluenceArcane matters realm-wide

History

When wild magic threatened to tear the world asunder, the great mages gathered to impose order upon chaos. The Collegium emerged from this crisis, dedicated to the responsible study and regulation of the arcane arts.

Structure

The Archmage Council leads, with ranks progressing from Apprentice through Journeyman, Adept, and Master. Each school of magic has its own Tower and traditions. Membership requires demonstrated talent and years of study.

Activities

Trains and certifies mages. Researches new magical applications. Regulates dangerous magic. Advises rulers on arcane matters. Hunts rogue practitioners who threaten public safety.

The Shadowveil

Compiled by Guildmaster Archivist Tobias Ledger

FoundedUnknown - claims ancient origins
HeadquartersUnknown - rumored multiple locations
InfluenceCovert, extensive

History

Whispered of in taverns and feared in palaces, the Shadowveil is the realm's premier guild of thieves and assassins. Whether they truly trace their origins to an ancient shadow cult or merely cultivate the mystique remains unknown.

Structure

Cell-based organization prevents any member from betraying more than their immediate circle. Leadership shrouded in mystery. Entry requires sponsorship and completion of proving tasks. Betrayal punished by death.

Activities

Theft, assassination, espionage, and blackmail - all for appropriate fees. Maintains strict code against certain targets. Operates sophisticated information network. Some whisper they serve darker masters.

The Knights of the Dawn

Compiled by Guildmaster Archivist Tobias Ledger

FoundedThird Age, Year 89
HeadquartersDawnhold Citadel
InfluenceMilitary and religious

History

Born from the chaos following the Void Incursion, the Knights of the Dawn dedicated themselves to defending the realm against darkness in all forms. They serve Elarion directly, wielding both sword and sacred light.

Structure

Grand Master commands from Dawnhold. Chapters established in major cities. Ranks progress from Squire through Knight, Knight-Captain, and Knight-Commander. Entry requires proof of valor and pure intent.

Activities

Combat void creatures and undead. Protect pilgrims and sacred sites. Investigate dark magic. Provide martial training to worthy candidates. Sometimes clash with secular authorities over jurisdiction.

The Naturalists Circle

Compiled by Guildmaster Archivist Tobias Ledger

FoundedFirst Age - exact date lost
HeadquartersThe Great Grove
InfluenceRural areas and wilderness

History

Since the world was young, druids have gathered in sacred groves to honor nature and maintain balance. The Naturalists Circle formalizes this ancient tradition, connecting practitioners across vast distances.

Structure

Archdruid Council convenes at solstices and equinoxes. Regional circles operate independently. No formal ranks - status earned through wisdom and service. Membership open to all who hear nature's call.

Activities

Protect wild places from exploitation. Maintain balance between civilization and nature. Preserve ancient knowledge of plants and animals. Mediate conflicts between settlements and wilderness. Guard against corruption of the natural order.

The Chroniclers

Compiled by Guildmaster Archivist Tobias Ledger

FoundedSecond Age, Year 512
HeadquartersThe Great Library of Eldoria
InfluenceAcademic and historical

History

When knowledge began slipping away, lost to war and neglect, the Chroniclers arose to preserve it. They copy manuscripts, interview witnesses to events, and compile histories that might otherwise be forgotten.

Structure

Grand Librarian oversees the Great Library. Senior Chroniclers manage regional archives. Juniors travel collecting information. Associate members contribute without formal commitment.

Activities

Preserve written works through copying. Record oral histories. Maintain archives accessible to scholars. Authenticate documents. Occasionally suppress dangerous knowledge deemed too perilous for general access.
