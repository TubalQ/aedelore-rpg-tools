Void Creatures

Shadow Stalker

Type: Void-spawned predator
Habitat: Areas where the Boundary is thin, ruins, deep caves
Threat Level: High

Description
Shadow Stalkers exist in the space between shadows, emerging only to feed on the life force of living creatures. They appear as darker patches within darkness itself-a shadow that moves against the light, that reaches when it should retreat.

Behavior
These creatures are patient hunters. They will follow prey for days, waiting for the moment when their target is alone, tired, or distracted. They do not kill for sustenance in the traditional sense-they drain vitality, leaving victims weakened, aged, or simply... absent. Bodies are rarely found.

Combat
Shadow Stalkers are vulnerable to direct light, particularly fire consecrated to Taninsam. They cannot manifest in brightly lit areas and will flee from sudden illumination. In darkness, however, they are nearly impossible to detect until they strike.

Lore
For it is written: the Shadow Stalker is not truly alive, for it was never born. It is hunger given form, a fragment of the Voids eternal appetite that has learned to hunt. Where one appears, others often follow-drawn by the weakening of reality that their presence causes.

Void Wraith

Type: Consumed soul
Habitat: Sites of mass death, corrupted temples, the Sunken City
Threat Level: Moderate

Description
Void Wraiths are the echoes of souls that were not merely killed but entirely consumed by the Void. Unlike ghosts, which retain some connection to their former lives, Wraiths remember nothing of what they once were. They exist only to serve the darkness that unmade them.

Behavior
Wraiths serve as scouts and heralds for greater Void entities. They drift through the world seeking cracks in the Boundary, potential hosts for corruption, or simply spreading despair through their mere presence. Those who see a Wraith often report overwhelming feelings of hopelessness and the certainty that all effort is futile.

Combat
Physical weapons pass through Void Wraiths harmlessly. Only magic, blessed weapons, or sacred flames can harm them. Their touch drains warmth and hope-prolonged contact can leave permanent psychological scars.

Lore
The Conclave of Wardens believes that Wraiths can be released through specific rituals of purification, freeing whatever fragment of the original soul remains. However, such rituals are dangerous and rarely attempted.

Entropy Weaver

Type: Reality-dissolving entity
Habitat: Tears in the Boundary, ruins of Ambers Call
Threat Level: Extreme

Description
Entropy Weavers are among the most dangerous Void entities that can manifest in Aedelore. They exist to unmake-dissolving matter and magic alike into raw nothingness. Where they pass, reality itself grows thin, creating permanent weak points in the Boundary.

Behavior
Unlike predatory Void creatures, Entropy Weavers do not hunt. They simply move, and everything they touch ceases to exist. They are drawn to concentrations of magic and life, perhaps because these represent the antithesis of the Void they serve.

Combat
Direct engagement with an Entropy Weaver is suicide. The only effective strategy is containment-using wards to redirect its path away from populated areas until it eventually dissipates or returns to the Void. The Arcane Creed maintains protocols for such containment, but even these are not always successful.

Lore
It is believed that Entropy Weavers are not sent by the Void but simply emerge when the Boundary weakens sufficiently. They are symptoms of corruption rather than its agents-a sign that reality itself is beginning to fail.

Whisper Fiend

Type: Corruptor
Habitat: Near sleeping mortals, places of despair
Threat Level: Moderate (individually), Extreme (long-term)

Description
Whisper Fiends are small, nearly invisible entities that specialize in corruption rather than direct harm. They appear as distortions in the air, barely perceptible movements at the edge of vision, voices that seem to come from inside ones own head.

Behavior
These creatures attach themselves to vulnerable mortals-those grieving, despairing, or simply exhausted-and begin their work. They whisper doubts, suspicions, and dark thoughts, so subtly that victims believe these thoughts are their own. Over time, the whispers grow louder, more insistent, until the victim begins to act on them.

Combat
Whisper Fiends flee from direct confrontation. They can be driven off temporarily by loud noise, bright light, or the presence of multiple alert individuals. Permanent removal requires identifying the fiends anchor point-usually an object or location associated with the victims trauma-and purifying it.

Lore
The tragedy of Whisper Fiends is that their victims often do not realize they are being influenced until it is too late. Many acts of inexplicable cruelty, betrayal, or despair have been traced to these insidious entities.

Void Maw

Type: Void beast
Habitat: Deep caves, abandoned mines, the depths of Ambers Call
Threat Level: High

Description
The Void Maw is a massive, worm-like creature that burrows through earth and stone, leaving tunnels of corrupted matter in its wake. Its body is a cylinder of absolute darkness, and where its head should be is only a circular opening-a maw that consumes everything it touches.

Behavior
Void Maws are drawn to sources of life and heat beneath the earth. They have devastated Dwarven mining operations and are believed to be responsible for several tunnel collapses that were initially blamed on natural causes. They do not seem intelligent in any meaningful sense-they simply consume.

Combat
The hide of a Void Maw is resistant to conventional weapons. Fire and light-based magic can harm them, but their size makes them difficult to kill quickly. The safest approach is to collapse their tunnel and seal them in, though this does not kill them-merely contains them.

Lore
Some scholars believe Void Maws are what remains when an Entropy Weaver partially fails to return to the Void-a fragment of unmaking that has taken on a more stable, if no less dangerous, form.

Formless Terror

Type: Unformed Void matter
Habitat: Tears in the Boundary
Threat Level: Moderate (individually), Extreme (in numbers)

Description
Formless Terrors are masses of pure Void-stuff that have not yet coalesced into specific shapes. They appear as roiling clouds of darkness, constantly shifting, occasionally forming eyes or mouths that dissolve as quickly as they appear.

Behavior
These creatures are driven purely by instinct-the Voids hunger translated into mindless action. They flow toward life like water toward low ground, engulfing and consuming anything organic they encounter. They are most dangerous in numbers, where they can overwhelm defenders through sheer volume.

Combat
Formless Terrors are vulnerable to all forms of light and fire. Even torchlight causes them to recoil. They can be destroyed by sustained magical assault, but the true danger is that killing one often attracts others. Where the Boundary is weak enough for Formless Terrors to emerge, there are always more waiting.

Lore
Some theorize that all Void creatures begin as Formless Terrors, slowly developing into more specialized forms as they feed and grow. If true, every Terror destroyed is a future nightmare prevented.

Voidling Swarm

Type: Lesser Void spawn
Habitat: Near thin points in the Boundary
Threat Level: Low (individually), High (as swarm)

Description
Voidlings are the smallest manifestations of the Void-tiny, skittering things no larger than a hand, appearing as small patches of animate darkness. Individually, they are nearly harmless. In swarms of hundreds or thousands, they can strip flesh from bone in moments.

Behavior
Voidlings function as a collective, moving and attacking in coordination without any apparent communication. They are drawn to warmth and will infiltrate buildings through the smallest cracks, emerging at night to feed. Their presence often indicates a nearby weakness in the Boundary.

Combat
Fire is the most effective weapon against Voidling swarms-even mundane fire will cause them to scatter. Area-effect magic can eliminate large numbers at once. The challenge is preventing their escape, as survivors will inevitably return with more of their kind.

Lore
Farmers and villagers in remote areas have developed various folk remedies against Voidlings, from lines of salt to hanging iron charms. The effectiveness of these methods varies, but the Voidlings clear reluctance to cross certain thresholds suggests they have some basis in truth.

Abyssal Watcher

Type: Void observer
Habitat: High places, watchtowers, anywhere with clear sightlines
Threat Level: Low (directly), High (strategically)

Description
Abyssal Watchers are stationary Void entities that manifest as floating spheres of darkness, each containing a single, unblinking eye. They do not attack, do not feed, do not corrupt-they simply observe, transmitting what they see back to the greater consciousness of the Void.

Behavior
Watchers appear in locations that offer broad views of populated areas. They remain in place for days or weeks, observing patterns of movement, defenses, and daily routines. Their presence often precedes a more significant Void incursion, suggesting they serve a scouting function.

Combat
Watchers will flee if approached aggressively, dissolving into shadow and reforming elsewhere. They can be destroyed by concentrated magical attack, but this often proves futile-another Watcher will simply appear to take its place. The Conclave recommends monitoring Watchers rather than destroying them, as their behavior can reveal Void intentions.

Lore
There is debate about whether Watchers are independent entities or extensions of a single vast intelligence. If the latter, every Watcher destroyed teaches the Void something about mortal defenses.

Corrupted One

Type: Void-twisted beast
Habitat: Varies based on original creature
Threat Level: Variable

Description
Corrupted Ones are ordinary animals that have been twisted by prolonged Void exposure. Their forms become distorted-extra limbs, eyes in wrong places, bodies that bend in impossible ways. Their behavior becomes aggressive, predatory, and seemingly driven by malice rather than instinct.

Behavior
Unlike other Void creatures, Corrupted Ones retain some memory of their original nature. A corrupted wolf still hunts in packs; a corrupted bear still defends territory. But these behaviors are twisted toward cruelty-they kill not for food but seemingly for pleasure, and they target intelligent prey over easier alternatives.

Combat
Corrupted Ones are tougher and more aggressive than their uncorrupted counterparts, but they remain vulnerable to the same weapons. Fire and blessed weapons are more effective than normal. The true danger is their unpredictability-corruption seems to grant them an unsettling cunning.

Lore
The sight of a Corrupted One often causes more psychological damage than physical. To see a creature that was once natural twisted into something hateful reminds observers of what the Void truly wants: not destruction, but perversion-the corruption of everything good into something terrible.

Undead

Draugr

Type: Animated corpse
Habitat: Ancient tombs, barrows, battlefields
Threat Level: Moderate

Description
Draugr are corpses that have risen from death, animated by rage, regret, or dark magic. Unlike mindless zombies, Draugr retain fragments of their former intelligence and often guard the places where they died with single-minded determination.

Behavior
Most Draugr are bound to specific locations-their tomb, their home, or the site of their death. They will not pursue intruders beyond these boundaries but will attack anything that enters with terrible ferocity. Some Draugr can speak, though their words are usually threats or laments.

Combat
Draugr are stronger than they were in life and feel no pain. They can be destroyed by dismemberment, fire, or blessed weapons. Silver is particularly effective. They are vulnerable to sunlight, which forces them to retreat to darkness.

Lore
The faithful of Tatsu consider Draugr a tragedy-souls that refused to accept his guidance and now exist in a tortured half-state. Proper funeral rites prevent Draugr rising; their existence often indicates disturbed graves or deaths so violent that the soul could not find peace.

Skeletal Warrior

Type: Animated skeleton
Habitat: Battlefields, crypts, sites of mass death
Threat Level: Low to Moderate

Description
Skeletal Warriors are the animated remains of fallen soldiers, stripped of flesh but still capable of wielding the weapons they carried in life. They move with an unsettling precision, their empty eye sockets somehow tracking living prey.

Behavior
Unlike Draugr, Skeletal Warriors have no individual will. They follow the commands of whoever raised them or, lacking a master, simply attack the living on sight. They fight with whatever tactics they knew in life, making well-trained warriors more dangerous even in death.

Combat
Skeletal Warriors are fragile compared to Draugr-a solid blow can shatter bones. However, they feel no fear and will not retreat. Bludgeoning weapons are most effective; edged weapons often pass between bones without doing significant damage.

Lore
The creation of Skeletal Warriors is forbidden by every major religion, as it denies the dead the rest they are owed. Necromancers who raise such forces are hunted by the Conclave of Wardens and the faithful of Tatsu alike.

Wraith

Type: Vengeful spirit
Habitat: Sites of murder, betrayal, or great injustice
Threat Level: High

Description
Wraiths are the spirits of those who died with such overwhelming hatred or grief that they refused Tatsus call. They appear as translucent, twisted versions of their living forms, their faces frozen in expressions of rage or anguish.

Behavior
Wraiths are driven by a single overriding purpose-usually vengeance against whoever wronged them in life. They will destroy anyone who stands between them and their target, but they have no interest in random violence. Some Wraiths have pursued their prey for centuries.

Combat
Physical weapons pass through Wraiths without effect. Only magic, blessed weapons, or weapons made of silver can harm them. Their touch drains life force, and prolonged contact can be fatal. Sunlight weakens them significantly.

Lore
A Wraith can only find rest when its purpose is fulfilled or when the injustice that created it is somehow addressed. Some heroes have found ways to give Wraiths peace without fulfilling their murderous intentions-but such solutions require understanding what the Wraith truly needs, which is rarely what it claims to want.

Ghost

Type: Lingering spirit
Habitat: Anywhere with strong emotional residue
Threat Level: Low to Moderate

Description
Ghosts are the spirits of the dead who remain in the mortal world due to unfinished business, strong emotional attachment, or simple confusion. Unlike Wraiths, most Ghosts are not malevolent-merely lost, sad, or unable to accept their deaths.

Behavior
Most Ghosts are harmless, appearing briefly to loved ones or replaying moments from their lives in endless loops. Some are aware of the living and can communicate; others seem oblivious, trapped in memories that no longer correspond to reality.

Combat
Hostile Ghosts are rare but dangerous. They can possess the weak-willed, create terrifying illusions, or drain life energy through touch. However, most Ghosts can be dealt with through negotiation, helping them complete their unfinished business, or performing proper funeral rites.

Lore
The faithful of Tatsu view Ghosts with compassion rather than fear. Each Ghost represents a soul that needs help finding its way-and helping a Ghost pass on is considered a sacred duty.

Revenant

Type: Returned warrior
Habitat: Pursuing their killers
Threat Level: High

Description
Revenants are warriors who have returned from death with a singular purpose: to destroy those who killed them. Unlike other undead, Revenants retain their full intelligence, skills, and memories. They appear as they did in life, though closer inspection reveals the wounds that killed them.

Behavior
A Revenant will pursue its target across any distance, through any obstacle, for as long as it takes. It cannot be bargained with, cannot be deterred, cannot be distracted. It exists only to kill, and once its purpose is fulfilled, it returns to death peacefully.

Combat
Revenants fight with the skill they possessed in life, making experienced warriors particularly dangerous. They can be destroyed through combat, but they will reform within days unless their killer is slain. Only fulfilling their vengeance-or destroying the killer by other means-ends their existence permanently.

Lore
Revenants are rare because they require a specific combination of circumstances: a skilled warrior, killed unjustly, with the will to return. The faithful of Tatsu view them as exceptions to the natural order-souls so wronged that even the god of death granted them time to seek justice.

The mightiest of giantkind, Storm Giants stand over thirty feet tall with purple or green-tinged skin. They command lightning and waves, dwelling in palaces beneath the sea or atop coastal cliffs. They are the nobility of giants.

Behavior
Storm Giants are contemplative and wise, often spending centuries in meditation. They intervene in mortal affairs only when great evil threatens. Their judgment is respected by all giants.

Cyclops

Bestiary entry by Aldric Shadowmark, Grand Hunters LodgeClassification: Giant-kin
Habitat: Islands and coastal caves
Threat Level: Dangerous

Description
One-eyed giants with a talent for crafting, cyclopes are solitary shepherds and smiths. They stand fifteen feet tall, and their single eye grants them limited prophetic abilities. Their forged items carry inherent magic.

Behavior
Cyclopes prefer isolation, tending their flocks of giant sheep. They are not aggressive unless threatened or their property is stolen. Many have deals with local communities, trading crafted goods for supplies.

Combat
NotesTheir single eye is the obvious target but well-protected. Thrown boulders and shepherd crooks are devastating. Some possess minor divination - they may know you are coming. Reasoning with them often works.

Description
The smallest of giant-kin at ten feet tall, Verbeegs are cunning and cowardly compared to their larger cousins. They resemble lanky, ugly humans and often work as scouts or servants for true giants.

Behavior
Verbeegs prefer trickery and ambush over direct confrontation. They bully creatures smaller than themselves while groveling before the strong. Many serve as intermediaries between giants and smaller folk.

Combat
NotesMore intelligent than they appear. They set traps and use poison. Expect them to flee if outmatched, then return with reinforcements. Can be bribed or intimidated.

Troll Brute

Habitat: Any dark places
Threat Level: Very Dangerous

Description
When trolls grow exceptionally old and well-fed, they become Brutes - massive specimens standing fifteen feet tall with regeneration so powerful they can regrow from near-complete destruction. They lead troll warbands and claim the best feeding grounds.

Behavior
Troll Brutes are driven by insatiable hunger. They dominate lesser trolls through violence and control hunting territories. Some display disturbing cunning, setting ambushes and raiding strategically.

Combat
NotesRegeneration even more powerful than normal trolls. Fire and acid still work but must be applied continuously. They can reattach severed limbs or grow new ones. Overwhelming force required.

Cave Troll

Bestiary entry by Aldric Shadowmark, Grand Hunters LodgeClassification: Trollkin
Habitat: Deep caves and underground
Threat Level: Dangerous

Description
Adapted to life in eternal darkness, Cave Trolls have pale, nearly translucent skin and heightened senses. Larger and slower than forest trolls, they compensate with even more powerful regeneration and stone-like hide.

Behavior
Cave Trolls are ambush predators, waiting motionless for prey to wander past. They have claimed entire cave systems, picking off explorers one by one. They fear bright light, which burns their sensitive skin.

Combat
NotesLight magic causes them pain and slows regeneration. Fire and acid still work. Their stone-like hide resists piercing weapons. In their home caves, they know every shadow and passage.

Sea Troll

Bestiary entry by Aldric Shadowmark, Grand Hunters LodgeClassification: Trollkin
Habitat: Coastal waters and underwater caves
Threat Level: Dangerous

Description
Also called Scrags, these aquatic trolls have webbed claws and gills alongside their lungs. Their scales are blue-green and they can regenerate even underwater. They raid coastal villages and attack ships.

Behavior
Sea Trolls lurk beneath the waves, attacking boats from below or snatching fishermen from docks. They drag prey underwater to drown before eating. Some form loose tribes in underwater caves.

Combat
NotesTheir regeneration works only in water - on land they are normal trolls. Fire is difficult to use underwater; acid remains effective. They retreat to water when wounded.

Once beautiful fey giants, Fomorians were cursed for their vanity. Now hideously deformed, their twisted bodies reflect their inner corruption. They dwell in the Underdark, plotting revenge against the fey who cursed them.

Firbolg

Bestiary entry by Aldric Shadowmark, Grand Hunters LodgeClassification: Giant-kin
Habitat: Deep forests
Threat Level: Low (unless provoked)

Description
Gentle forest giants standing twelve feet tall, Firbolgs live in harmony with nature. Their blue-tinged skin and bovine features make them distinctive. They possess innate druidic magic and can speak with animals and plants.

Behavior
Firbolgs avoid contact with other races, preferring their forest homes. They are peaceful unless their woods are threatened, at which point they become fearsome defenders. Some form friendships with druids and rangers.

Combat
NotesTheir nature magic makes forests fight alongside them. They can turn invisible in natural settings. Prefer negotiation but fight effectively when necessary. Harming their forest home brings the full tribe.

Oni

Bestiary entry by Aldric Shadowmark, Grand Hunters LodgeClassification: Giant-kin Spirit
Habitat: Near humanoid settlements
Threat Level: Dangerous

Description
Blue or green-skinned giant-kin with horns and tusks, oni are shapeshifters who infiltrate humanoid society. They can assume any humanoid form and use this ability to position themselves for feeding on mortals they have grown to trust.

Behavior
Oni are patient predators. They integrate into communities, earning trust before revealing their nature and feasting. They prefer positions of authority and enjoy the terror of betrayal as much as the meal itself.

Combat
NotesShapeshifting makes them hard to identify. They regenerate like trolls. Flight and invisibility provide escape options. Their true form is powerful in combat. Iron weapons prevent regeneration.
